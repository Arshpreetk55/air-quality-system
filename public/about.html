About AirSense

Know your air, as it happens

AirSense is a real-time air quality monitor built around a low-cost sensor and a live dashboard. It reads particulate levels, temperature, and humidity every few seconds and turns them into something anyone can understand at a glance — not just a number, but a clear read on whether the air around you is safe to breathe.

Our purpose

Air quality shouldn't be a mystery

Most people only think about air quality when it's visibly bad — smoke, haze, a stuffy room. By then it's often already affected how they feel. AirSense exists to close that gap: a sensor quietly measures the air where you actually spend time, and the dashboard makes the reading immediate and legible, using the same color language as public air quality indexes so a glance tells the whole story.

It started as a small hardware project — an Arduino, a particulate sensor, and a Firestore database — and grew into a full monitoring system with live readings, historical trends, configurable alert thresholds, and a running log of every time the air crossed into unhealthy territory.

The sensor

An Arduino-driven particulate sensor samples the air continuously and streams readings over serial to a local bridge.

The cloud

Every reading is written to Firestore in real time, so the dashboard, history, and alerts all stay in sync.

The people

A clear dial, a live trend, and configurable alerts — built so anyone can understand it, not just engineers.
